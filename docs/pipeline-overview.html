Competitive HCP Communication Monitoring

How the pipeline works — from LLM_VALIDATION to the final report

Goal. Find which doctors (HCPs) actually said something about a competitor drug (Wirkstoff / brand), capture their exact words grounded in the source, and report it — so every statement is trustworthy, not a false attribution (e.g. a doctor merely named on a page where the drug appears elsewhere).

The pipeline runs in five stages. The key decision happens at Stage 2, where each competitor follows one of two tracks depending on where it came from: cf from the client's Content-Frame spec, or llm from the model's general knowledge.

Stage 1 — Identify competitors

1

From the client drug + Content-Frame terms → a competitor list

An LLM maps the client's Content-Frame terms (CONTENT_FRAME_SPEC) and its own knowledge to concrete competitor drugs. Each competitor is tagged with its origin — cf (in the spec) or llm (knowledge only). This tag selects the Stage 2 track. The indication (e.g. Adipositas) is taken authoritatively from the spec, never from the model's guess.

Stage 2 — Retrieve source documents (the split happens here)

cf Track A — uses LLM_VALIDATION

  1. Filter (the gate): keep LLM_VALIDATION rows where NEAR_BY=1, IS_OLD=0, IS_DOCTOR=1 and the brand/generic appears in COL_KEYWORDS_ORIG/EN. (IN_RELATION is deliberately ignored — not reliable.) → relevant pages + mapped HCPs (real records, S_CUSTOMER_ID).
  2. Vector search within those pages only → best ~100 passages per drug.
  3. Assemble the entire content of the matched documents.

llm Track B — no LLM_VALIDATION

  1. LLM_VALIDATION offers nothing here, so it is skipped entirely.
  2. Vector search across the whole corpus for the drug → best ~100 passages.
  3. Assemble the full documents.

No LLM_VALIDATION link means no mapped HCPs on this track — every doctor found is “not mapped”.

Stage 3 — Build the knowledge (the “LLM-wiki”) — same for both tracks

3

Extract → ground → verify → map

1. Extract — an LLM pulls out only statements where a named doctor genuinely expresses a view about the drug (not merely mentioned). 2. Ground — the doctor's quote must appear word-for-word in the source, or it is discarded (this kills false attributions). 3. Verify — a second, independent LLM check confirms the doctor really said it about that drug. 4. Map — match the doctor to a known record → mapped (S_CUSTOMER_ID attached) or not mapped (Track B is always here).

Stage 4 — Synthesize

4

Aggregate the verified statements per competitor

Sentiment (positive / negative / neutral / mixed) split by mapped vs. not-mapped doctors, plus a short narrative per competitor and an overall summary.

Stage 5 — Report

5

Three HTML reports + a full Excel export

Business, plain-language, and technical reports show 10–15 example statements per section (with a “+N more — see Excel” note), each flagged mapped / not-mapped and linked to its source. Competitor headings show the Wirkstoff in brackets (e.g. Saxenda (Liraglutid)), and a cross-competitor section highlights doctors who weigh in on several competitors. The Excel holds every statement.

Bottom line. LLM_VALIDATION is used only for cf competitors, and purely as a starting filter — to find the right pages and the mapped doctors. It never decides what a doctor “said”. The trustworthiness comes from Stage 3, where every statement must be quoted verbatim from the source and independently verified before it can appear in the report.
